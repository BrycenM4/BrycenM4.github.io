Project Narrative

The artifact I chose to enhance for the Software Design and Engineering category was my CS-330 Computational Graphics and Visualizations 3D scene. This project is an OpenGL application written in C++. The original version of the project consisted of a simple desktop scene containing a pencil, eraser, piece of paper, and a coffee cup, along with a basic lighting system to add visual depth. I completed this project during the previous term, and after reflecting on it, I identified several areas where it could be expanded and improved, which led me to select it for enhancement in this course.

This artifact demonstrates my ability to think creatively by visualizing and constructing an idea within a three-dimensional space. Projects of this nature require significant fine-tuning and adjustment, which highlights my patience and persistence throughout the development process. For this enhancement, I transformed the original desktop scene into a complete room environment that includes windows, a door, a chair, and lighting fixtures. I also improved the lighting system to give the scene a more realistic look and feel.

I believe that I successfully met the course outcomes selected in Module One by improving the organization of my code and adding more comments, which enhanced code readability and communication. Additionally, this project demonstrates my ability to apply innovative techniques and technical skills to bring a conceptual vision to life within a fully realized 3D scene.

Overall, the process of enhancing this artifact was both challenging and rewarding. I was able to apply the experience and knowledge gained from the original project to support further enhancements. The workflow remained similar to the original development process, involving the addition of mesh objects, application of textures, and adjustments to lighting. One of the greatest challenges was properly positioning objects within the scene, particularly when working with dozens of interconnected elements. Lighting also presented difficulties, as limitations in the lighting system required creative adjustments to achieve a visually appealing result. Despite these challenges, the project significantly expanded my understanding of 3D design, particularly in creating full-scale scenes that require careful coordination of objects, textures, and materials.
